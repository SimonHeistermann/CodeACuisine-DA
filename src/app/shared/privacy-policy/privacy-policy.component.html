3. Data We Collect

We process personal data that you provide to us or that is automatically collected when you use this application.

3.1 Recipe Generation Data

When you generate recipes, the following data is transmitted to our backend service for processing:

  • Ingredient names, serving sizes, and units of measurement
  • Cooking preferences (cooking time, cuisine, diet preferences)
  • Number of portions and number of cooks

This data is sent to an n8n workflow hosted on Render.com, which forwards it to an AI model (Google Gemini) for recipe generation. The generated recipes are then stored in our database.

3.2 Recipe Storage Data

Generated recipes are stored in Google Firebase Firestore. Stored data includes:

  • Recipe titles, ingredients, and cooking directions
  • Cooking preferences and nutritional information
  • Like/favorite counters (aggregated, not linked to individual users)
  • Recipe signatures (for deduplication)
  • Creation timestamps

3.3 Favorites (Local Storage)

When you mark a recipe as a favorite, this preference is stored exclusively in your browser's localStorage. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to our servers. You can clear it at any time through your browser settings.

3.4 Technical Data

When you visit this website, the following data may be automatically collected by our hosting provider:

  • IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Date, time, and duration of access
  • Pages visited

9. Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access (Art. 15 GDPR) — You may request confirmation of whether we process your personal data and, if so, obtain a copy of that data.
  • Right to rectification (Art. 16 GDPR) — You may request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure (Art. 17 GDPR) — You may request deletion of your data, subject to legal retention obligations.
  • Right to restriction (Art. 18 GDPR) — You may request restriction of processing in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability (Art. 20 GDPR) — You may request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object (Art. 21 GDPR) — You may object to processing based on legitimate interests at any time, for reasons related to your particular situation.
  • Right to withdraw consent (Art. 7(3) GDPR) — Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at simon@heistermann-solutions.de. We will respond within one month of receiving your request.
